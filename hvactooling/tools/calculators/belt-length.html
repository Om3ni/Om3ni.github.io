▶ How to Measure Pulleys
Method 1 — String Wrap (No Tools Required)
  1. Wrap a piece of string or wire around the pulley in the groove where the belt sits — not the outer rim
  2. Mark or cut the string where it meets itself
  3. Lay the string flat and measure the length — this is the pitch circumference
  4. Divide by π (3.14159) to get the pitch diameter
Most accurate field method without calipers. Works on any sheave regardless of number of grooves.
Method 2 — Calipers (Most Accurate)
  1. Measure across the outside diameter (OD) of the pulley with calipers
  2. Measure the groove depth from the outer rim down to the bottom of the V-groove
  3. Pitch diameter = OD − (2 × groove depth)

Method 3 — Tape Measure (Quick Estimate)
  1. Measure straight across the outer rim of the pulley with a tape measure — this is the OD
  2. Use the depth gauge on your tape measure housing (or a small ruler) to measure the groove depth
  3. Pitch diameter = OD − (2 × groove depth)
Less precise than calipers but usually good enough for belt matching. If in doubt, round to the nearest ¼".
Center Distance
  1. Measure from the center of one shaft to the center of the other shaft
  2. If you can't access the shafts, measure edge-to-edge between pulleys and add half of each pulley's OD
If you have the old belt and it's not stretched: lay it flat, measure the inside length, and enter that directly as pitch length instead of using the calculator formula.
Sheave Stamping Shortcut
Check the sheave for a stamped part number — many manufacturers stamp the pitch diameter or a catalog number on the hub or rim. Common formats: AK41 (A section, 4.1" PD), BK52 (B section, 5.2" PD). If you can read the stamping, you don't need to measure.
