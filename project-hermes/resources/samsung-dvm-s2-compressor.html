Samsung DVM S2 Compressor Replacement

AM192BXVGJR/AA  ·  16-Ton Heat Pump Module  ·  R-410A  ·  460V 3-Phase
Primary: AC-00294E §3-2-6/§3-2-7 (NA Service Manual) Tech Bulletin: TB2017-0001 PVE Oil — DB81-02598A EEPROM: DB82-06811A  ·  Inverter PBA: DB92-05092C
Pre-Work Safety & Requirements
⚠ RTM ⚠

Read all instructions prior to beginning repair. Failure to do so may result in damage to equipment and voiding of the warranty. A link to the manual is provided at the end of this document. The navigation and special operations tables are located at the bottom of the document. Beneath each step is the section of the manual it refers to. Steps marked with the purple badge apply only to 2-compressor models. Steps marked with the red badge indicate critical safety operations that require extra attention. Steps marked with the yellow badge indicate operations that require specific tools or conditions.

⚠ Recovered Confirmation Before Any Heat Source ⚠

Confirm all refrigerant has been reclaimed on both high and low side before cutting or applying heat to any pipe connection. Make sure that accumulator has not frozen and is not trapping refrigerant. After Reclaim steps run vacuum for at least 20 minutes prior to opening system. Make sure system was placed into recovery mode. if it was not you will need to begin again.

⚡ DC Bus Voltage — 15-Minute Minimum Wait

After power-off, wait at least 15 minutes before accessing the inverter PBA or compressor terminals. DC link capacitors remain charged after disconnect. Note: if E464 or E364 has been active, power devices may be damaged; do not use the DC link discharge mode in this condition. Read to the table and refer to the manual if you are confused by this step. Wait for natural discharge only.

✂ Pipe Separation — Ratchet Cutter Preferred

The NA service manual specifies cutting discharge, VI, and suction ports using a ratchet cutter only. Traditional cutters work fine, but you don't get the close off pinch that you would with a ratchet cutter. Tape all cut surfaces immediately to prevent oil overflow and foreign matter ingress.

★ 2-Compressor Unit — Steps 2 & 10 Specific to This Model

The AM192BXVGJR/AA contains two compressors. Step 2 (Heating Pump Out / refrigerant release) and Step 10 (adjacent compressor oil evaluation) apply only to 2-compressor models and are marked throughout.

Compressor Exchange Procedure — AC-00294E §3-2-7
01
Identify & Designate Failed Compressor
Confirm which compressor is to be replaced. Note compressor 1 vs compressor 2 position before beginning disassembly. Before any mechanical work, check the accum oil return and oil return pipes for blockage — temperature detection through the front panel gaps while the system is still powered is the recommended method.
AC-00294E §3-2-7 Step 1 / §3-2-6 No.6
02
Heating Pump Out — Refrigerant Release 2-Comp Only K1 × 3–6
Run a Heating Pump Out operation to migrate refrigerant from the indoor units and piping back to the outdoor unit before recovery. This is what the service manual refers to as "refrigerant release driving."
  • Initiate by pressing K1 3 times (outdoor unit address 1). Use 4, 5, or 6 presses for addresses 2, 3, 4 in a modular system
  • Compressor runs at 60 Hz, 4-way valve ON (heating mode), outdoor fan at maximum, main EEV at 700 steps on operating side
  • Maximum operation time: 10 minutes
  • Monitor low pressure via K4 view mode. If low pressure drops below 0.2 MPa·g: immediately close the gas side service valve — pump out shuts down automatically. Press K1 once more or K3 once to abort manually
  • If low pressure drops below 0.1 MPa·g: system stops automatically to protect the compressor
  • The first pump out must complete fully before running a second pump out. Back-to-back pump outs without allowing the first to complete is cited as a direct cause of compressor damage
AC-00294E §4-2-1 Heating Pump Out / §3-2-7 Step 2 — 2-comp models only
03
Close All SVC Valves
Lock all service valves on both the liquid pipe and gas pipe sides.
AC-00294E §3-2-7 Step 3
04
Enter Vacuum Mode — Open All EEVs & Valves K1 × 7–11
Press K1 7 times (outdoor unit address 1) to open all outdoor unit EEVs and solenoid valves — this is the correct vacuum mode entry for this unit per the NA service manual K1 table. Use 8, 9, 10 for addresses 2–4. Press 11 times to open all outdoor units simultaneously in a modular system.
  • Compressor, indoor units, and outdoor fans all stop in vacuum mode
  • 4-way valve goes OFF
  • Normal operation is blocked until vacuum mode is turned off — this prevents accidental restart during service
AC-00294E §4-2-1 Vacuum Operation / §3-2-7 Step 4
05
Recover Refrigerant Recovery Unit Required
Reclaim all refrigerant from the outdoor unit. If no recovery unit is available:
  • After the pump out, approximately 1.5 kg typically remains in the outdoor unit — more in cold weather as refrigerant accumulates in the accumulator
  • Reference the factory refrigerant charge label on the outdoor unit for total system charge
  • Use the S-Checker refrigerant quantity decision function to calculate the recharge amount needed at Step 15
  • S-Checker information can be found around page 330 of the Service Manual under Test Operation.
  • Record all quantities — new R-410A only at recharge; do not reuse recovered refrigerant
AC-00294E §3-2-7 Step 5
06
Power Off at Circuit Breaker
Open the MCCB/circuit breaker feeding the outdoor unit. Wait minimum 15 minutes for DC bus capacitor discharge before accessing any electrical components.
AC-00294E §3-2-7 Step 6
07
Remove Failed Compressor Manifold Verify First
Confirm zero pressure on both sides via manifold gauge before proceeding. Then:
  • Remove power terminal block cover. 90cc compressors have additional soundproofing felt on the terminal block — remove felt first
  • Unfasten 3 terminal screws on U, V, W power cables and disconnect leads. Torque on reassembly: 180-250-ft lbs — loose terminals are a fire hazard
  • Separate velcro soundproofing felt and remove from compressor body
  • Remove top thermistor from compressor
  • Cut discharge, VI, and suction ports using a ratchet cutter at marked cutting points — no saws or grinders
  • Tape all cut surfaces immediately to prevent oil overflow and contamination ingress
  • With compressor body freed, use torch to separate any remaining welded pipe stubs
AC-00294E §3-2-6 / §3-2-7 Step 7
08
Weigh Failed Compressor & Measure Oil
Weigh the pulled compressor and drain and measure the oil quantity remaining inside it. Compare against the known new compressor weight from the reference table below.
  • Oil addition formula: Oil to add (lbs) = Weight of failed compressor − Weight of new compressor
  • Maximum oil addition capped at 2.2 lbs (1 kg) regardless of calculated difference
  • If remaining oil in failed compressor is below 0.7 lbs (0.3 kg): oil circuit blockage is the likely root cause
  • If failed compressor is more than 1.8 lbs (0.8 kg) lighter than new: inspect accumulator, ARV, and oil separator for blockage — blockage probability is high. Resolve before installing new compressor
  • Per TB2017-0001 (confirmed applicable): if delta ≥ 1.75 lbs, find and repair the oil circulation fault before installing new compressor — failure to do so will cause repeat compressor failure
AC-00294E §3-2-7 Step 8 & Check Points p.50 · TB2017-0001
09
Check Oil Color — ASTM D1500
Evaluate drained oil against ASTM D1500 color scale.
  • Grades 1–2.5: Oil acceptable — proceed with single compressor replacement
  • Grade 3 or higher: Oil contaminated — evaluate adjacent compressor before proceeding (see Step 10)
AC-00294E §3-2-7 Check Points p.50
10
Evaluate Adjacent Compressor 2-Comp Only
If oil from Step 9 is ASTM grade 3 or worse, measure oil quantity and color of the adjacent compressor and determine whether it also requires replacement before proceeding. If oil circuit blockage was identified in Step 8, service the accumulator, ARV, or oil separator as needed before new compressor installation.
AC-00294E §3-2-7 Step 10 — 2-comp models only
11
Determine Oil Addition Quantity
Using the weight difference from Step 8, determine the volume of oil to add to the outdoor unit. Use the calculator below. Maximum addition is 2.2 lbs (1 kg).
AC-00294E §3-2-7 Step 11 / Check Points p.50
12
Install New Compressor & Add Oil
Install the new compressor. Reconnect all pipe joints with nitrogen-purged brazing. Reconnect U, V, W power leads — torque terminal screws to 180-250 ft·lbs. Reinstall thermistor, soundproofing felt, and terminal block cover. Add the calculated oil quantity to the outdoor unit.
  • Per TB2017-0001 (confirmed applicable): install a suction shell & core type filter on the suction line
  • Use Samsung PVE oil — service code DB81-02598A (1L can)
AC-00294E §3-2-7 Step 12 · TB2017-0001 suction filter
13
Restore Power — Re-Enter Vacuum Mode K1 × 7–11
Reconnect power at the circuit breaker. Press K1 7 times (address 1) again to re-open all EEVs and internal valves in preparation for the evacuation sequence.
AC-00294E §3-2-7 Step 13
14
Pressure Test with Nitrogen → Evacuate
  • Pressure test with dry nitrogen and verify no loss
  • Release all nitrogen from the outdoor unit completely
  • Per TB2017-0001 (confirmed applicable): triple evacuate to < 500 microns — hold for minimum 1 hour at final pull
AC-00294E §3-2-7 Step 14 · TB2017-0001 evacuation spec
15
Weigh In New Refrigerant New Charge Only — Do Not Reuse
Add the refrigerant quantity determined in Step 5 using the factory label or S-Checker. New R-410A only. Weigh in — do not charge by pressure.
  • Per TB2017-0001 (confirmed applicable): in cold weather, add a liquid line drier
  • Per TB2017-0001 (confirmed applicable): if heating mode must be run during break-in, put the suction line drier in bypass mode
AC-00294E §3-2-7 Step 15 · TB2017-0001 drier notes
16
Open SVC Valves — Auto Trial Operation K1 press & hold
Open all service valves. Initiate Auto Trial Operation by pressing and holding K1 on the outdoor main PCB. Display shows K · K · — · — when active. Auto Trial runs a minimum of 60 minutes, maximum 10 hours. System will display UP (UnPrepared) until trial operation completes — normal operation is blocked until this clears.
AC-00294E §3-2-7 Step 16 / §4-2-1 Auto Trial Operation

⚠ DC Link Discharge Mode (K2 × 6) — Critical Restrictions

Do not use DC link discharge mode if errors E464 or E364 are active — power devices may be damaged and discharge mode will not function correctly. Do not use if a capless inverter is installed. If discharge mode cannot be used, wait at least 15 minutes for natural capacitor discharge before touching any inverter or fan PBA components.

Post-Installation Break-In & Verification
ℹ Source — TB2017-0001 (Confirmed Applicable)

The 48-hour break-in and acid test protocol below is drawn from Samsung Technical Bulletin TB2017-0001. This bulletin has been confirmed applicable to the DVM S2 platform including the AM192BXVGJR/AA. Confirm with Samsung HVAC tech support (800-726-8263) if documentation is required for warranty purposes.

Operate system in Cooling Only mode for 48 hours after commissioning Cold weather exception: if heating is required, add liquid line drier and put suction line drier in bypass mode · TB2017-0001
At 48 hours: perform refrigerant acid test and inspect suction filter cartridge core for acid and particulates Oil test must confirm suitability for PVE oil · TB2017-0001
If acid or particulates found: replace filter core and re-inspect at the next 48-hour interval Repeat until acid-free result confirmed. Do not sign off until clean · TB2017-0001
Verify oil cycle operation using temperature differential method ARV normal: disconnect temp − connect temp > 9°F  |  Oil separator normal: check point = discharge temp − 27–36°F  |  Accumulator normal: check point = low pressure saturation temp · TB2017-0001
Confirm system EEPROM data is intact or re-enter via S-Net Pro2 EEPROM chip is removable and stores startup info, serial number, and field-programmed settings. Verify EEP code DB82-06811A is loaded for AM192BXVGJR/AA · AC-00294E §4-2-2
Confirm Auto Trial Operation completes and UP display clears System displays UP (UnPrepared) after compressor replacement. Normal operation is blocked until Auto Trial Operation completes successfully — minimum 60 minutes, maximum 10 hours · AC-00294E §4-3-1
