▶ How to Use Psychrometric Analysis
When to Use Each Mode
Single State — quick read of one air sample. Confirm room RH, check outdoor conditions, sanity-check a stat reading.

System Diagnostic — full air-handling analysis with RA, SA, optional OA + MA. Use for coil performance verification, economizer evaluation, and mixed-air diagnostics.
Where to Measure
  • RA — Return Air, at the return grille upstream of the evaporator
  • SA — Supply Air, in the supply duct or closest register downstream of the evaporator
  • OA — Outdoor Air, at the outdoor unit or the outside-air intake on a commercial RTU
  • MA — Mixed Air, in the mixing chamber after the OA dampers, or use Calculate from OA% if you know the damper position
Measurement Tips
  1. Allow the probe 2–5 min to equilibrate before reading, especially when moving from outdoor air to post-coil air
  2. SA is typically 90–95% RH — readings will look near saturation. This is normal.
  3. Watch for condensate forming on the probe body when you first plant it; wipe and re-read after a moment
  4. Capacitive RH probes (Testo 435/440 class) read accurately at high RH; consumer-grade meters do not
If the WB depression (Tdb − Twb) is under 1°F, you're either on a high-performance coil or your probe needs to settle longer.
Reading the Coil Process Numbers
SHR (Sensible Heat Ratio) — fraction of cooling that's temperature change vs moisture removal:
  • > 0.85 — mostly sensible (dry climate or high CFM/ton)
  • 0.65–0.85 — typical mixed cooling
  • < 0.65 — heavy dehumidification (humid climate or dehumidify mode)
Bypass Factor — portion of air passing the coil without contacting the surface:
  • < 0.10 — high-performance deep-slab coil
  • 0.10–0.20 — typical residential / light commercial
  • > 0.25 — shallow coil or fouling indicator
ADP (Apparatus Dew Point) — effective average coil surface temperature. Compare to design ADP from equipment spec to verify the coil is doing its job.

Capacity in tons — total BTU/h ÷ 12,000. Compare to nameplate; significant shortfall indicates low charge, dirty condenser, restricted airflow, or undersized unit for the load.
Target RH & Comfort Zone
Target RH sets a goal humidity for the space. The chart draws a dotted blue curve at the target RH and a delta line from the RA state showing the moisture gap. Numerical delta is reported in % RH and gr/lb, with humidify / dehumidify direction.

Show Comfort Zone overlays the ASHRAE 55-2020 cooling and heating polygons on the chart. If the RA state lands inside, the space is in comfort range. If outside, you have a customer narrative for what's wrong.
Economizer Evaluation
Available when both OA and RA are entered.
  • Enthalpy Check (preferred) — h_OA < h_RA means free cooling is available; bring in OA
  • Dry-Bulb Check — simpler but less accurate; can call for OA when it's actually wetter than RA
  • High-Limit Lockout — ASHRAE 90.1 requires shutting off OA above 28 BTU/lb regardless of differential
